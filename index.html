About


Michael J. Fox is an actor, author, producer and activist.

He is best known for his roles in Spin City (1996 - 2000), Family Ties (1982 - 1989) and Back to the Future (1985 - 1990).

Fox was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease in 1991, at age 29, which caused him to retire from his career of acting. But Fox remained strong, continuing his work as a voice-over actor (also gave voice to Stuart Little) and created the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's disease research.

To me Michael J. Fox remains a hero forever who played Marty McFly and his struggle and will to make a change keeps me motivated to push forward.

Back to the Future


Fox plays the role of a teenager Marty McFly in the movie who is accidentally sent back in time.

Back to the Future was both a commercial and critical success. The film spent 8 consecutive weekends as the number one grossing movie at the US box office in 1985 and eventually earned a worldwide total of $381.11 million.

BTTF is my personal favorite and a must watch movie for all.


" The more I expect, the more unhappy I am going to be. The more I accept, the more serene I am.
- Michael J. Fox


Fox started displaying symptoms of early-onset Parkinson's disease in 1991 although he was not properly diagnosed until the next year.

In 1998, he decided to go public with his condition, and since then, he has been a strong advocate of Parkinson's disease research.

His foundation, The Michael J. Fox Foundation, was created to help advance every promising research path to curing Parkinson's disease, including embryonic stem cell studies.
